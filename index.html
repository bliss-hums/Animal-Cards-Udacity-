African Elephant

The ears of African elephants are described as being shaped like the African continent.

  • Scientific Name: Loxodonta Africana
  • Food: Plant Material
  • Habitat: Savannah
  • Lifespan: 60-70 years

African elephants are the largest land animals on Earth. African elephants are keystone species, In the forest, their diet creates pathways for smaller animals to move through, and in the savanna, they help keep the landscape open for zebras and other plains animals to thrive.

Indian Cobra

A cobra's eyesight allows it to see a moving person almost 100m away.

  • Scientific Name: Naja Naja
  • Means of Birth: Oviparous
  • Habitat: Forests and Swamps
  • Lifespan: 24 years

It can grow up to 18 feet long. And the animal's toxic bite is powerful enough to kill an elephant. Cobras eat small mammals, lizards, and birds.
